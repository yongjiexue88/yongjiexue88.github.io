# Advanced Vocabulary for Simple English Phrases

Date: 2026-05-13  
Category: english-writing

## Summary

This note collects advanced vocabulary that can replace simple phrases such as "very good," "very bad," "not clear," or "required." The goal is to make writing and speaking more precise, natural, and reviewable.

## Key Points

- Many "very + adjective" phrases can be replaced with one stronger word.
- Some advanced words are formal, sensitive, or context-specific, so usage matters.
- The list is grouped by meaning to make future review easier.

## Decisions / Conclusions

- Save this as an English vocabulary review note in the `english-writing` category.
- Use grouped tables instead of one long raw list.

## Open Questions

- None.

## Next Steps

- Review one section at a time.
- Practice by writing example sentences for the words that feel unfamiliar.

## How to Review

1. Cover the `Advanced vocab` column and try to recall the word from the simple phrase.
2. Read the meaning and create one sentence.
3. Mark words that feel hard and review only those next time.

## Sensitive Or Context-Specific Words

| Word | Why to be careful |
| --- | --- |
| obese | Usually medical and can sound rude if used casually about a person. |
| destitute | Strong word for extreme poverty, not just "not rich." |
| infamous | Negative fame only. Do not use it for someone famous in a good way. |
| derogatory | Used for insulting language, especially toward people or groups. |
| amateur | Can be neutral, but can also sound insulting if used to judge someone's skill. |
| delinquent | Can mean overdue payment or young criminal behavior, depending on context. |

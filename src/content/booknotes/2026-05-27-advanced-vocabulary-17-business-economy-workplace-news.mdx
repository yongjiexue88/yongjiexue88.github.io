## Usage Notes And Common Mistakes

| Topic | Note |
| --- | --- |
| adopt vs adopting | At the beginning of a sentence, use the gerund: `Adopting this new technology will improve the bottom line.` |
| desire vs desires | Use singular if you mean one main desire: `My desire for success outweighs my fear of failure.` |
| outweighs | With singular subject `desire`, use `outweighs`, not `outweigh`. |
| experienced | Say `an experienced skier`, not `a very experience skier`. `Experienced` is the adjective. |
| banter | `Banter` can be a noun or a verb: `office banter`; `we bantered with them`. |
| slam | `Slam` can mean criticize harshly or close something loudly. Context decides the meaning. |
| steep | `Steep` can describe a mountain/slope or a very high price. |
| address pronunciation | As a verb, `address` usually stresses the second syllable: ad-DRESS. As a noun, it usually stresses the first syllable: AD-dress. |
| little hope | `There is little hope` means almost no hope, so it sounds negative. |
| a little hope | `There is a little hope` means some hope, so it sounds more positive. |

## Sensitive Or Context-Specific Words

| Word / phrase | Why to be careful |
| --- | --- |
| gospel | Can relate to religion, but in business/news it often means something treated as absolute truth. Use carefully depending on context. |
| angst | Describes inner anxiety or emotional tension. It is not the same as `daunting`, which describes something difficult or intimidating. |
| job cuts | A softer/business-news way to say layoffs, but it still refers to people losing jobs. |
| severance | Only use when talking about layoff or termination pay, not normal salary. |
| fuel | Often means making a bad situation worse, such as fueling anger, inflation, or conflict. |
| slam | Can sound strong because it means harsh criticism. |

## Traffic And Infrastructure Key Concepts

- Road-condition vocabulary includes `snarled`, `lane closures`, `motorists`, `commute`, `westbound`, and `stretch`.
- Bridge-repair vocabulary includes `hinge`, `railing`, `storm drain`, `spokesperson`, and `strenuous`.
- Formal expressions include `take place`, `subject to change`, `closed through the weekend`, and directional words with `bound`, such as `westbound`.

# On not knowing what I want

I keep trying to answer the question *what do I want next?* in one sitting. As if a long enough walk or a clean enough notebook will produce a paragraph that finally sounds like me.

It hasn't been working.

So I am trying a different thing: letting the question stay open. I don't have to resolve it this week. I don't have to resolve it before my birthday. I don't have to resolve it just because someone asked at dinner.

The honest answer right now is *I don't know yet, and I'm paying attention.* That is allowed to be the answer.

> Some questions are not problems to solve. They are weather to live through.
